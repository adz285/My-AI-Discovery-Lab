Indexing ≠ Interpretable

You’re SEO-visible. You’re structurally invisible to AI.

Most catalogues render fine for humans, but the machine-readable layer is incomplete or ambiguous — which breaks comparison, filtering, and recommendation.

  • Generic Product schema across catalogue
  • Variant offers collapsed into ambiguity
  • Attributes buried in product copy
  • Duplicate schema conflicts from apps
  • No validation or drift monitoring
What this means in practice

AI systems infer entities and constraints. If offers, attributes, and identifiers aren’t structured and consistent, products become harder to retrieve and easy to ignore.

Common failure mode

A single Offer representing multiple variants creates pricing and availability ambiguity — even when you’re “indexed”.

Fix = per-variant offer architecture + identifier continuity.

The infrastructure layer we build

Outputs-first. No hype. This is catalogue architecture, implemented and validated.

Lab process (validation-first)

We treat your catalogue like a data system — not a set of pages.

  1. 1. Extract — live product JSON + rendered JSON-LD

  3. 3. Implement — template output changes (theme/app-aware)

FAQ

Will this break my theme? +

No. We work at the structured output layer (templates + injected schema). Changes are isolated, testable, and reversible.

How do you handle app-injected schema? +

We identify duplicates and conflicts, then suppress or de-conflict app output where possible and establish a single authoritative entity strategy.

How do you model variants? +

One product entity, many offers: per-variant Offer arrays with stable identifiers, URLs, price, currency, and availability. No collapsed ambiguity.

How do you prevent drift? +

Validation checks plus monitoring. When apps, themes, or product data change, we detect regressions and report what broke and what to fix.

What platforms do you support? +

Shopify, WooCommerce, headless, and custom storefronts. The structured layer is the same: entity clarity, offer logic, attributes, identifiers, and validation.
